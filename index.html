Dr. Svenja Guhr

About

Svenja Guhr is a postdoctoral researcher at the fortext lab and has an academic background in German literature, Romance linguistics, and Digital Humanities. As a computational literary studies scholar, her PhD project focused on the operationalization and analysis of sound and loudness as narratological phenomena in German-language literary prose of the 19th and early 20th century.

Book Project

Raise Your Voice! - Character Sound in German-Language Fiction

Sound is omnipresent in our everyday lives and also in literary texts, fictional sounds create the soundscapes of fictional worlds.

In July 2024, I signed my book contract with Metzler/Springer Nature for the publication of my monograph Raise Your Voice!. It will be published in the book series "Digitale Literaturwissenschaft" ("Digital Literary Studies") in Spring 2025. It will be a gold open-access publication that I could realize thanks to the financial support of Women in Science of the Humanities Department of the Technical University of Darmstadt, the Association of Digital Humanities in the German-speaking area, and the TU Darmstadt Open Access Publication Fonds for English publications.

Projects at the fortext lab, Technical University of Darmstadt, Germany

theme-d-Prose

The corpus "theme-d-Prose 1848-1920" is a collection of 1.227 German-language literary prose texts published between 1848 and 1920. It is an extended subcorpus of "d-Prose 1870-1920" (Gius/Guhr/Adelmann 2021), which is the source of 804 of its text files. In terms of social and literary history, the selected publication period (1848-1920) covers a time of many political changes in the German-speaking area with a diverse literary production that can retrospectively be grouped into the following major literary movements and epochs: the end of pre-March and the Biedermeier, Realism, Naturalism, Modernism, and Expressionism. The corpus is published on Zenodo (Version 1.0 from July 2024).

d-Prose

The corpus “d-Prose 1870-1920” is a heterogeneous collection of 2.511 German-language literary prose texts published between 1870 and 1920. It contains narrative texts from trivial and high literature with a minimum length of 1000 words. The corpus was created from manually and semi-automatically selected texts from the German-speaking area. The corpus is published on Zenodo (Version 2.0 from June 2021).
